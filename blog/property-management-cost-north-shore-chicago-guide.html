How Much Does Property Management Cost in North Shore Chicago? (2025 Guide)

Financial Planning • September 18, 2025 • 10 min read

Short Answer

At Manage369, pricing is a flat monthly fee per unit, customized after a property walkthrough based on your community's size, systems, and service needs. We do not charge a percentage of rent, and we do not manage apartments or rentals—only HOAs, condominiums, and townhome associations.

Key Takeaways

  • Flat per-unit fee (no % of rent) and line-item scope tailored after a walkthrough
  • Built for HOA/Condo/Townhome associations—no apartments or rentals
  • Pricing reflects unit count, building complexity, amenities, service level, and North Shore conditions (snow/ice, older building stock)
  • Transparent proposals with what's included vs. optional—no surprise add-ons
  • Backed by 18+ years in Chicagoland, IDFPR-licensed (#291.000211), CAI/IREM/CMCA/CCIM credentials

How Manage369 Prices Association Management

We start with a site walkthrough and a review of your governing docs, vendor contracts, loss history, and reserve plan. Then we provide a fixed, flat per-unit monthly fee with a clearly defined scope.

Your proposal shows:

  • Included services (financials, meetings, communications, maintenance coordination, 24/7 response)
  • Optional services (capital project management, on-site support, special projects)
  • One-time onboarding items (document transfer, owner portal rollout, vendor setup) if applicable

Why flat per-unit? Association management is driven by board governance, reserves, and building systems—not rent. A per-unit model aligns work with the real workload and keeps billing predictable.

What Drives Cost on the North Shore

  • Number of Units & Property Type – Mid-rise vs. townhome vs. high-rise; single building or multiple entrances.
  • Mechanical Complexity – Boilers/hydronic heat, elevators, cooling towers, generators, life-safety systems.
  • Amenities & Common Areas – Garages, roofs/terraces, fitness rooms, doorking/gate systems, pools.
  • Service Level – Financial-only vs. full service; meeting cadence; owner communications volume.
  • Compliance & Documentation – Insurance requirements, vendor COIs, Illinois condo/HOA compliance.
  • Seasonal Demands – Snow/ice oversight, freeze prevention, storm response unique to the lakefront suburbs.

What's Included in the Base Per-Unit Fee

  • Financial Management: monthly board packets on time; budget vs. actuals; AR/AP; reserve tracking.
  • Board & Governance: agendas, minutes, action items, and IL compliance guidance.
  • Owner Communications: email/SMS/portal notices; issue tracking and follow-through.
  • Maintenance Coordination: work orders, vendor scheduling, COIs, and performance oversight.
  • 24/7/365 Emergency Response: direct line (not a call center) and pre-vetted North Shore contractors.
  • Reporting & Transparency: open-book accounting and audit-ready records.

Common Optional/Project Services (Scoped Upfront)

  • Capital Project Management (roofing, façade, MEP upgrades) with a pre-agreed fixed or time-and-materials fee
  • On-Site Support (e.g., turnover days, inspections, staffing coordination)
  • Policy & Governance Projects (rule updates, special assessment administration)
  • Technology & Owner Engagement (portal migrations, custom notices/campaigns)

No surprises: optional items are quoted in advance and approved by the board before we proceed.

North Shore Reality Check: Why Fees Vary

The North Shore combines older building systems, stringent village processes, and heavy winter operations. Communities in Wilmette, Winnetka, Glencoe, Highland Park, Glenview, Northbrook, Evanston, Lake Forest, and Lake Bluff often require more hands-on seasonal planning and vendor oversight—factors that influence total management effort (and therefore per-unit pricing).

Comparing Quotes: Make It Apples-to-Apples

Use this quick checklist when evaluating proposals (including ours):

  • Flat per-unit fee with a written scope (no % of rent, no vague "packages").
  • Named day-to-day manager, caseload, and escalation path.
  • Sample monthly packet (financials + variance + reserve view).
  • 24/7 plan with response targets and local vendor list.
  • Onboarding timeline (first 30 days) and owner communication plan.
  • Optional services listed separately with pre-agreed fees.

If a proposal is cheap but vague, it's usually expensive later.

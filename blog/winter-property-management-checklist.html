Why Winter Prep Matters on the North Shore

Skipping or delaying winterization often leads to:

  • Frozen or burst pipes and costly water damage
  • Boiler or furnace failures during cold snaps
  • Slip-and-fall liability from inadequate snow/ice control
  • Unplanned special assessments due to emergency repairs

üè¢ Building Systems Checklist

Heating & Boilers

  • Schedule pre-season inspections (combustion analysis, safeties, relief valves).
  • Verify circulator pumps, mixing valves, expansion tanks, and glycol levels.
  • Test thermostats, building controls, and boiler lead/lag sequences.
  • Stock critical spares (igniters, belts, filters) and confirm vendor response times.

Domestic Water & Pipe Protection

  • Insulate domestic and hydronic lines in mechanical rooms, garages, and perimeter walls.
  • Heat-trace freeze-prone runs (garage risers, exterior hose bibbs, stairwells).
  • Post cold-weather drip guidelines for end units or vulnerable stacks.
  • Confirm isolation valves are operable for rapid shut-offs.

Generators & Life Safety

  • Load-test emergency generators; check fuel quality/levels.
  • Test CO/smoke detectors, fire alarm monitoring, and emergency lighting.
  • Review elevator entrapment procedures and emergency call routing.

Building Envelope

  • Inspect roofs for loose flashing, open seams, and clogged drains.
  • Clean gutters/scuppers; install or check ice-dam mitigation where needed.
  • Re-seal windows/doors and repair failed weatherstripping to limit heat loss.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Snow & Ice Management Plan

Contracts & Scope

  • Lock in a 24/7 contract with clear triggers (e.g., 1" snowfall), response times, and de-icing requirements.
  • Define priority routes: fire lanes, ADA access, main entries, mail/package areas.

Materials & Storage

  • Stage salt/ice melt/sand and secure on-site bins.
  • Choose materials appropriate for concrete and pet safety; document application logs.

Risk & Insurance

  • Collect vendor COIs naming the association as additional insured.
  • Keep incident logs and signage for temporary hazards; photograph conditions after storms.

üì£ Emergency Communication & Continuity

  • Maintain multi-channel notifications (email/SMS/robocall/portal) for weather alerts.
  • Publish a cold-weather action plan: thermostat settings, water shut-off instructions, and parking reminders for plowing.
  • Create a utility outage SOP: generator run times, stairwell lighting, warming areas, and elevator procedures.
  • Keep an updated directory of board contacts, vendors, and emergency services.

üí∞ Budgeting & Reserve Planning (Board-Ready)

Plan winter costs in the annual budget and reserves. Typical line items include:

  • Snow & Ice Removal: varies widely by footprint/contract structure; budget for heavier seasons.
  • Heating System Service: annual tune-ups plus contingency for unexpected repairs.
  • Emergency Contingency: set aside 15‚Äì20% for weather-related incidents.
  • Supplies: salt, ice melt, safety cones, signage, filters, belts, and small tools.

Tip: Align winter spending with your reserve study and long-term replacement plan (roofs, boilers, controls). Preventive spend usually costs less than emergency response.

üìë Documentation & Compliance

  • Centralize service logs, inspection reports, and winterization checklists.
  • File and track COIs, W-9s, permits, and vendor licenses.
  • After each storm or incident, complete a post-event report with photos and timestamps.

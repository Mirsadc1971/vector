Why Your North Shore HOA Insurance Jumped in 2025 (and How to Push It Back Down)

Published: September 18, 2025 | By: Manage369 Expert Team

Reality check: Illinois property insurance has been rising—driven by severe convective storms, higher repair costs, and tighter underwriting. For example, State Farm implemented an average 27% homeowners increase in Illinois in mid-2025 and lifted condo/unit-owner rates by ~10.8% (plus new 1% wind/hail deductibles).

At the (re)insurance layer, 2025 renewals were less brutal than 2023–24: capacity improved and pricing eased at 1/1 and mid-year, but carriers are still passing through prior loss pressure and adjusting deductibles/terms—especially in hail-exposed Midwest markets.

What North Shore boards are seeing: many associations faced low-double-digit to ~20% premium moves in 2025; larger spikes happen at buildings with recent losses, high replacement costs, or tighter lender requirements—but "40–60% across the board" is not the norm this year.

What's Pushing Premiums Up

  • Severe weather losses (hail/wind) in IL raising claim frequency/severity
  • Replacement-cost inflation (labor/materials) and higher loss ratios
  • Reinsurance pass-throughs & tightened terms after heavy 2023–24 CAT years (now stabilizing, but not fully reversed)

Proven Ways Boards Can Fight Back

1. Market Early & Block the Market

Market early (120–150 days) and "block the market" with a condo-specialist broker; demand multiple quotes and side-by-side term sheets. (Terms matter as much as price.)

2. Right-Size Deductibles

Consider separate wind/hail deductibles (percentage or higher flat) to buy down premium—model the payback using your loss history.

3. Update Valuations

Commission a current replacement-cost appraisal; over- or under-insuring both waste money. Industry capacity in 2025 often supports better terms if values are accurate.

4. Tune Coverage

Review Ordinance or Law, water ingress/sublimits, equipment breakdown, and director & officer limits—trim what you don't need, fill critical gaps you do. (Illinois condo statutes leave many unit betterments to owners—align master vs. HO-6.)

5. Loss-Control Wins

Document roofs/electrical/plumbing upgrades, leak sensors, sump alarms, and contractor COIs; submitting a clean risk-improvement memo can materially improve quotes.

6. Claims Hygiene

Avoid "frequency" dings—raise small-loss deductibles, use board-approved vendors, and close open claims fast with documentation.

7. Consider Alternatives

Layered programs, higher primary retentions, or group/captive solutions for larger portfolios when economics justify it.

Update for 2025

Many North Shore HOAs saw ~10–20% premium pressure this year; larger jumps occur at properties with recent losses or higher risk profiles.

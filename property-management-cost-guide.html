Understanding Property Management Costs in Chicago

When evaluating property management costs in Chicago, it's essential to understand that fees vary significantly based on property type, location, and service level. Our 18+ years of experience managing 50+ properties across Chicago and the North Shore gives us unique insight into fair market pricing.

Factors Affecting Property Management Costs

  • Property Location: Downtown Chicago properties typically command higher management fees (6-10%) due to increased complexity and higher property values. North Shore suburbs like Wilmette, Winnetka, and Highland Park average 5-8% due to lower operational costs.
  • Number of Units: Larger properties benefit from economies of scale. Properties with 100+ units often see per-unit costs 20-30% lower than smaller properties.
  • Service Level: Full-service management including 24/7 maintenance, financial reporting, and board support costs more than basic rent collection services.
  • Property Condition: Older properties requiring more maintenance coordination may incur higher management fees.
  • Market Conditions: Competitive markets with high tenant turnover may require more intensive management services.

What's Included in Professional Property Management?

Professional property management fees typically include:

  • Tenant screening and placement
  • Rent collection and enforcement
  • Maintenance coordination (labor costs separate)
  • Financial reporting and budgeting
  • Legal compliance and documentation
  • 24/7 emergency response
  • Vendor management and negotiations
  • Property inspections
  • Board meeting support (for associations)
  • Online owner and tenant portals

Hidden Costs to Watch For

When comparing property management companies, be aware of potential hidden fees:

  • Setup Fees: Many companies charge $500-2000 to onboard new properties. We waive all setup fees.
  • Lease Renewal Fees: Some charge 25-50% of monthly rent for renewals. We charge $0-200 maximum.
  • Maintenance Markups: Watch for companies that mark up vendor invoices by 10-20%.
  • Technology Fees: Monthly charges for "software" or "portal access" ranging from $10-50.
  • Vacancy Fees: Some companies charge fees even when units are vacant.
